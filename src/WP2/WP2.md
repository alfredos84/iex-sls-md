# WP2 

### Generate Ion-Exchange Glasses through Uniform Na→K substitution (“stuffed glass” approach) Goal: Quantify LNDC, mechanical evolution and AE for diffusion with exchange fraction. 

## Simulations 

- Replace Na by K in increments (e.g., 20%, 40%, 60%, 80%, 100%). All the other studies replaced 100% of Na in each composition to study LNDC. You could do both to see differences. 

- Relax at exchange temperature (∼700–750 K as you have already done, why did you choose these temperatures? We must justify). 

- Quench to 300 K. 

- Compute molar volume, elastic properties and AE for diffusion (for this you have to run long simulations 10 ns per different T) for each composition. 

## Analysis 

- Volume vs K fraction → compute LNDC from $$\partial V/\partial C$$ (see Mauro’s Papers). 

- Compare with linear interpolation of as-melted Na- and K-endmembers. 

- Elastic moduli vs K fraction. 

- Local structural evolution: Na–O, K–O, Ca–O coordination; site volume (Voronoi analysis). 

- Si–O–Si angle distributions, $$Q^n$$ and ring size distributions. Compare with as-melted glasses 

### The deliverable will be the computation and understanding of composition-dependent LNDC, evolution of the structure and properties in ion-exchanged soda–lime glasses.
